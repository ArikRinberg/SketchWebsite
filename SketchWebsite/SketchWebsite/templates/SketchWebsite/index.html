Processing high-speed, high-volume data often requires performing analytics on a representation of the data, rather than the data itself. To this end, data sketching algorithms have become an indispensable tool for high-speed computations over massive datasets. They maintain the streams' state and answer queries on it (e.g., how many unique elements are in the stream, what is the frequency of elements) using limited memory, at the cost of giving approximations rather than exact answers.
